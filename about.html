ABOUT TEDxCMU

WHAT IS TEDx?

In the spirit of ideas worth spreading, TEDx is a program of local, self-organized events that bring people together to share a TED-like experience. At a TEDx event, TED Talks video and live speakers combine to spark deep discussion and connection. These local, self-organized events are branded TEDx, where x = independently organized TED event.

The TED Conference provides general guidance for the TEDx program, but individual TEDx events are self-organized. (Subject to certain rules and regulations.)

OUR MISSION

TEDxCMU is Carnegie Mellon's premier platform for sharing transformative ideas. We bring together students, faculty, researchers, and community members to explore groundbreaking concepts that challenge conventional thinking and inspire action.

Through our main conferences, salon events, and ongoing story collection, we create spaces for authentic dialogue about the ideas shaping our world. From artificial intelligence and sustainable design to personal narratives of resilience and innovation, TEDxCMU celebrates the diversity of thought that defines the CMU community.

INNOVATION

We showcase cutting-edge research and unconventional thinking that pushes boundaries and opens new possibilities.

INCLUSION

We amplify diverse voices and perspectives, ensuring our platform represents the full spectrum of our community.

IMPACT

We believe ideas have the power to create real change, and we're committed to sparking conversations that matter.

OUR HISTORY

Since our founding, TEDxCMU has brought hundreds of speakers to our stage, from renowned researchers like Luis von Ahn (founder of Duolingo) and Subra Suresh (former NSF Director) to inspiring students sharing their personal journeys. Each event builds on our tradition of excellence while exploring new formats and themes.
